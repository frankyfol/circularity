# Circular City — Student Manual

How to play **Circular City** in class or in solo practice.

---

## What you are doing

You are the **Chief Sustainability Officer** of a growing city. Over **six years**, you make waste and sustainability decisions. Your city has five scores (pillars) that should stay in **balance**:

- Environment  
- Economy  
- Liveability  
- Capacity  
- Circularity  

After many choices, you answer a short **insight quiz**. Correct answers add **Insight** points. Your final rank depends on balanced pillars plus Insight — not on “always picking recycle.”

---

## Joining a class game (Streamlit)

Your teacher will give you a link to the app.

1. Open the link on your laptop or phone.
2. Go to the **Student join** tab.
3. Enter the **5-letter room code** from the board.
4. Enter your **name**.
5. Choose your **city type**:
   - **High-income** — more budget and consumption; high-tech options cost more.
   - **Low / middle-income** — tighter budget, faster growth; different strengths (e.g. informal recycling).
6. Click **Join room**.
7. Wait in the lobby until the teacher starts the game.

**In class, you do not choose quiz difficulty** — your teacher sets Easy, Standard, or Hard for everyone.

---

## Joining a class game (React app)

If your class uses the separate game website (not Streamlit):

1. Open the URL your teacher provides.
2. Click **Join as Student**.
3. Enter your name, room code, and city type.
4. Click **Found My City** and wait for the teacher to start.

---

## Playing each year

### Step 1 — Growth tick

Population and affluence increase. Read the message and click **Continue to events**.

### Step 2 — Events

Each year has several events (decisions). Typical order:

| Year | What you face |
|------|----------------|
| **Year 1** | **Founding charter** (same for every student) → 3 city events |
| **Years 2–6** | 3 city events → 1 **world event** (affects everyone) |

For **each event**:

1. **Read** the story on screen.
2. **Pick an action** (A, B, C, …). Each option shows a cost. Options are **shuffled** — read every choice carefully.
3. **Defer** (if shown) — spend nothing this event; you may still get a quiz.
4. Click **Confirm choice**.
5. **Insight quiz** — answer the multiple-choice question.
   - **Easy** = +2 insight if correct  
   - **Standard** = +4  
   - **Hard** = +6  
   (Your teacher sets the tier in class.)
6. Read the **result** and any explanation.
7. Click **Continue** for the next event.

### Step 3 — Year in review

When you finish all events in a year, open **Year in review**. It explains the **pros and cons** of what you chose. Use it to learn — the “greenest” option is not always best for your city that year.

### Step 4 — Wait for the class

The teacher advances to the next year when most students are done. Stay on the page; the game updates automatically (Streamlit refreshes every few seconds).

---

## After Year 6

You will see:

- Your **rank** in the class  
- **Balance score** (how even your five pillars are)  
- **Insight** points  
- A **report card** — your strongest and weakest pillar and a short verdict  

---

## Solo practice (Streamlit only)

For homework or revision:

1. Open the app → **Solo practice** tab.
2. Enter your name and city type.
3. Choose **quiz difficulty** (Easy / Standard / Hard) for yourself.
4. Click **Start solo game**.

In solo mode:

- Events are **random** each year (except the same **founding charter** in Year 1).
- **You** control the pace — no room code or teacher.

---

## How to play well (without ruining the surprise)

- **Balance matters** — ignoring a pillar (especially Capacity or Liveability) usually hurts your final score.
- **Watch your budget** — overspending can create debt and hurt Economy.
- **Think waste hierarchy** — reduce and reuse often help long-term, but may cost more now.
- **Read quizzes carefully** — Insight can reach up to **60** total and affects your rank.
- **No perfect strategy** — linear options are sometimes tempting; circular options are not always best in every situation. The year review explains why.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | What to do |
|---------|------------|
| Room not found | Check the code with your teacher (5 letters). |
| Stuck on “waiting for teacher” | Normal in the lobby — wait for **Start game**. |
| Screen seems frozen | Refresh the browser; re-join with the same name if needed. |
| Cannot afford an action | Choose a cheaper option or **Defer** if available. |
| No quiz appeared | Select an action first, then **Confirm choice**. |
| Wrong tab | Use **Student join**, not Teacher host or Solo (unless practising alone). |
